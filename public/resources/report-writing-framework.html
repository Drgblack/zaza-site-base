Section 1: Celebrate First (What's Going Well)

Opening Positive Statement:
[Student name] brings [specific positive quality] to our classroom community. This [quarter/semester], I've been particularly impressed by [specific achievement or behavior].

üí° Pro Tip

Start with something unique to this student. Avoid generic phrases like "good student" or "works hard."

Academic Strengths:
In [subject area], [student name] demonstrates [specific skill/strength]. For example, [specific evidence or example]. They consistently show [specific positive behavior or skill] when [specific situation].

Example

"In mathematics, Sarah demonstrates strong number sense and logical reasoning. For example, during our fraction unit, she was able to explain her problem-solving strategies clearly and help classmates understand difficult concepts. She consistently shows persistence when tackling multi-step problems."

Social/Emotional Strengths:
[Student name] shows [social/emotional strength] through [specific examples]. They contribute to our classroom community by [specific positive behaviors].

Section 2: Navigate Growth Areas (Where We're Headed)

Growth-Focused Goal Setting:
As we move forward, we're focusing on [specific area for growth]. [Student name] is ready to build on their strengths in [specific area] by [specific strategies or next steps].

üí° Pro Tip

Frame challenges as growth opportunities. Use language like "building," "developing," and "strengthening" rather than "struggling with" or "needs improvement."

Specific Academic Goals:
In [subject area], [student name] is working on [specific skill or concept]. They have shown progress in [specific area] and our next focus is [specific next step]. I'm providing additional support through [specific strategies].
Social/Emotional Development Goals:
We're working together to help [student name] develop [specific social/emotional skill]. They're making progress by [specific evidence of growth] and we're continuing to build on this through [specific strategies].

Example

"We're working together to help Marcus develop stronger organizational systems. He's making progress by consistently using his weekly planner and we're continuing to build on this through daily check-ins and visual reminders for long-term assignments."

Section 3: Partner Forward (How We'll Work Together)

Home-School Partnership:
I'd love to collaborate with you on [specific area] to ensure [student name] has consistent support. Based on what I'm seeing at school, I'm wondering [specific question about home observations or strategies].
Specific Recommendations:
To continue supporting [student name]'s growth, I recommend [specific actionable suggestions]. At home, you might consider [specific home support strategies].
Next Steps & Timeline:
Over the next [time period], we'll focus on [specific goals]. I'll monitor progress through [specific assessment methods] and will communicate with you about [student name]'s development through [specific communication plan].
Closing & Invitation:
I'm confident that [student name] will [specific positive outcome] with continued support. Please don't hesitate to reach out if you'd like to discuss [student name]'s progress in more detail or if you have insights to share about what works well at home.

Quick Reference: Power Phrases

For Academic Progress:

  • "demonstrates strong understanding of..."
  • "consistently shows growth in..."
  • "has mastered the skill of..."
  • "is ready to tackle..."
  • "shows excellent reasoning when..."

For Growth Areas:

  • "we're building..."
  • "developing strategies for..."
  • "working together to strengthen..."
  • "focusing on building confidence in..."
  • "ready to take the next step with..."

Final Checklist Before Sending

  • Does the report start with something genuinely positive and specific?
  • Are growth areas framed as opportunities rather than deficits?
  • Have I included specific examples and evidence?
  • Does the tone feel collaborative and supportive?
  • Are my recommendations specific and actionable?
  • Have I invited parent partnership and communication?
  • Would I feel good receiving this report about my own child?
