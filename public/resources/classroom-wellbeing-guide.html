Teacher Wellbeing in 10 Minutes a Day

Research-backed strategies to reduce burnout and reclaim your joy in teaching

Why 10 Minutes Matters

You don't need hours of self-care to make a real difference in your wellbeing. Research shows that small, consistent practices are more effective than sporadic large efforts.

Research Finding: Teachers who engage in just 10 minutes of intentional wellbeing practices daily show 23% less burnout symptoms and 31% higher job satisfaction after 6 weeks. (Educational Psychology Review, 2023)

The key: Consistency over intensity. Ten minutes you can actually maintain is better than an hour you'll abandon after a week.

Your Daily 10-Minute Wellbeing Menu

Choose one practice each day. Mix and match based on your energy level and what you need most.

üåÖ Morning Energy Builders (Before School)

2-Minute Morning Intention

Before checking your phone or email:

  • Take 5 deep breaths
  • Set one teaching intention for the day
  • Name one thing you're grateful for
  • Remind yourself: "I make a difference in kids' lives"
5-Minute Energy Boost

Pick your energy level:

  • Low energy: Gentle stretching or mindful tea drinking
  • Medium energy: Quick walk around the block
  • High energy: Dance to 2 favorite songs
3-Minute Classroom Prep Meditation

Sit in your empty classroom and:

  • Look around and appreciate your teaching space
  • Breathe deeply and visualize a successful day
  • Set an intention to be present with your students

The 5-Minute Nature Break
  • Step outside (or look out a window)

  • Write down one thing that went well this morning
  • Text a colleague about something positive
  • Take a moment to appreciate a student breakthrough
  • Acknowledge your teaching skills and impact

üåÜ Evening Transitions (After School)

The Teaching Day Closure Ritual

Before leaving school:

  • Spend 2 minutes tidying your desk mindfully
  • Write down three things that went well today
  • Set one clear intention for tomorrow
  • Mentally "close" your teaching day
  • Tell yourself: "My work today mattered"
Transition Breathing

In your car or walking home:

  • Take 10 slow, deep breaths
  • With each exhale, release the day's stress
  • With each inhale, welcome personal time
  • Arrive home truly present for your family/personal life
Creative 10 Minutes
  • Doodle, sketch, or color
  • Write in a personal journal (not school-related)
  • Play music and sing along
  • Do something creative just for joy

Quick Wins for Tough Days

When you're feeling overwhelmed, choose one of these 2-minute practices:

The 4-7-8 Breath

Inhale for 4 counts, hold for 7, exhale for 8. Repeat 3 times. This activates your parasympathetic nervous system and reduces stress hormones immediately.

The "Good Enough" Mantra

Repeat: "I am enough. My efforts are enough. My teaching is enough." Sometimes we need permission to be human.

The 30-Second Gratitude Practice

Name three specific things you're grateful for right now. Make them concrete: "warm coffee," "Sarah's smile when she understood fractions," "having a job that matters."

The Body Scan Check-In

Quickly scan your body from head to toe. Where are you holding tension? Consciously relax those areas. Your body stores stress - releasing it helps your mind follow.
